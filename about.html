What is Colonia Treze Against Cancer?

Colonia Treze Against Cancer was a project created by professors Marcos Matos and Roberto Junior, implemented in Colônia Treze, a community located in Brazil, in the state of Lagarto / SE, which took place between 2017-2018 and was composed of 11 students from local schools , these being: Anna Letícia Fernandes Barbosa Bispo, Eduardo Pereira Costa, Evely Julianne Anjos Santos, Ítalo Martins de Deus (me), Jéssica Anísia Lima dos Santos, José David Alves Sales, José Dhonatas Alves Sales, Maísa Fernanda dos Santos, Maísa Pocinio Souza, Maria Marcela Santos de Jesus and Vitória Pinto de Andrade.

Our objective was to investigate whether the cancer cases in Colony Thirteen are related to the use of pesticides. The motivation for this study was mainly the high number of cancer cases that occur in the region and the high consumption of pesticides in the community's crops.

During our work, we gave several lectures with the objective of making the population aware about the risks of pesticides and preventive measures against cancer, in addition to other activities, such as field research that was subjected to a static analysis by USP (University of São Paulo ) to determine whether there was a correlation between local cancer cases and the use of pesticides.
